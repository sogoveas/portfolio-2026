About

I design systems that make complex data visible, interpretable, and human.

I am a data science student at the University of San Francisco working at the intersection of computation, design, and systems thinking. My work focuses on building tools and experiences that translate complex data into structured, visual narratives.

Across both research and design, I am interested in how abstract systems — whether neural signals, social platforms, or digital identities — can be modeled, interpreted, and reimagined through code.

My recent work explores early autism detection using EEG data, combining nonlinear dynamical features with machine learning models to identify neural patterns before behavioral diagnosis.
